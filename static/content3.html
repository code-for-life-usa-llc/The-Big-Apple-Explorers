Landmarks

Landmarks are certain parts of a place that hold historical and cultural value.

Statue of Liberty

Lady Liberty, officially known as "Liberty Enlighting the World", was given to the United States as a gift from France in the 1870s. This statue represents a roman goddess and the crown represents the oceans and continents by using the spikes on it, The statue is located on Ellis Island, between New York and New Jersey. The statue is held by a steel frame and contains 62,000 pounds of copper. Furthermore, the man who built the Eiffel Tower helped make the statue as well.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building. which was built in 1930, is another extraordianry landmark in New York City. It took 13 months to build and its top was originally supposed to be an airport for blimps and zepplins. A fun fact about the Empire State Building is that it doesn't have a thirtenth floor. This was due to superstition that thirteen is an unlucky number which is otherwise known as triskaidekaphobia.

The Flatiron Building

The Flatiron Building was built in a year, being completed in the year in 1902. It's called the Flatiron because it looked like one of the flatirons that were used during the 20th century. Many people were scared that it would collapse due to its slender form but to this day it still stands as a historical landmark of New York City. The building is worth 190 million dollars and has benn in eleven movies.

Hotspots in NYC

Hotspots are places in NYC that are frquently visited.

Chinatown

Chinatown was founded in the 1870s as Chinese immigrants started arriving in large numbers. Furthermore, in the modern-day, it's become a home for chinese culture in NYC.From Buddha statues to wonderful cuisine and all the way to chinese medicine and art. You can find all of it here. By the same token, there are multiple Chinatowns in NYC, giving you the chance to experience it anywhere in New York. Locations: Manhattan, Queens, Broooklyn.

American Museum of Natural History

Further down the list of New York Hotspots, we have the Museum of Natural History. Founded in 1869, the museum grew rapidly with artifacts and exhibitions. Located at 200 Central Park W, New Youk, NY 10024, this place is wondorous. As stated by Britannica, "The museum conducts research in anthropology, astronomy, entomology, herpetology, ichthyology, invertebrates, mammalogy, mineralogy, ornithology, and vertebrate paleontology. The museum has a 485,000-volume library on natural history, as well as photo, film, and manuscript collections."

Times Square

Lastly, the most popular spot of NYC is Times Square. Times Square begain in 1898, originally known as Long Acre Square. It was orginally meant for William H. Vanderbilt's American Horse Exchange. Eventually the theater buisness arrvied in town and it began booming. However, during the Great Depression and World War 2, theaters were struggling so they began promoting explicit imagery to gain more customers. The drug trade also grew in the 70s around this area. However, it has become much safer and now its a place of dazzling lights, theater, and oppurtunity. Times Sqaure, located on 42nd Street has now become the capital of Broadway and the Theater sector of NYC.
